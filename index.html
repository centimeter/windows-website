Baggage Ramp

This was a final ME capstone project for 2.009. We traveled to the WestJet HQ and met with Delta. The project was a massive team effort and continued for several months after the class and demo. Photos are both from the class itself.

fpgaVr

This was for a class and actually won a department award. It was an fpga board that allowed you to navigate a little black and white world in VR. We used an SD card for memory and basically wrote a bunch of transformations that read different parts of the SD card directly to these screens, which I wrote a driver for.

Sit to Stand

This is a small robot I constructed for a class, designed to investigate the mechanics of sit to stand in populations with muscle weaknesses. We ran optimizations to find optimal sit to stand trajectories. I was primarily responsible for hardware but did some of the controls work as well.

Phone Booth

Designed this mock phone booth for a group of local activists interested in increasing civic engagement in their community. The booth is designed to be modular, lightweight, and can be assembled and disassembled quickly and on the spot. I will post updates if one is ever built physically.

Twisted String Actuator

This is my current area of research--stay tuned for updates!

Continuum Actuator

I did some work on continuum based robots at one point. The goal was to build devices that had the benefits of both rigid and soft exoskeletons. This device looked cool but never progressed beyond early prototypes.

Amira Terra

During covid, some friends and I started building an in bed cooling device that can predict and prevent hot flashes for menopausal women. This turned into a startup, and a product called the Amira Terra that is available today at amira.care. As a cofounder, I worked on hardware and some operations for the startup.
